The Cathedral Hill Historic District

A notable neighborhood collection of mixed-use, low-, middle-, and upper-income historic buildings.

Links and Documents

Original Application (saved on MO State Parks site)

The residential, commercial, and institutional structures built in Cathedral Hill include a wide variety of ninetheenth and twentieth century plan types and archtectural styles. There are center hall plan cottages, shotgun cottages, composite cottages, four-squares, bungalows, gable and wing cottages, and many others. A wide variety of architectural styles were used as well, including Greek Revival, Italianate, Second Empire, Romanesque Revival, Queen Anne, Craftsman, Colonial Revival, Tudor Revival, and Minimalist Traditional, along with combinations and variations of these styles. This reflects the dramatic growth, both population and economic growth, experienced in St. Joseph during the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries.
