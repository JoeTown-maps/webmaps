The Robidoux Hill Historic District

This district epitomizes the 19th and early 20th century aspiring middle class.

Links and Documents

Original Application (saved on MO State Parks site)

Lying on the hilltops overlooking the commercial heart of the city, the district epitomizes the pattern of aspiring middle class housing. The majority of the houses where built as Victorian single-family residences intermixed with late ninetheenth century duplexes. A number of residences feature carriage houses. The majority of these are constructed of brick and mimic the style of the period.

The distinctiveness of the district lies primarily in the retention of the physical characteristics associated with Victorian era family housing.

Select Houses and Buildings from the original application

524 N. 6th

A two-story Greek Revival house built ca. 1865 for local merchant Joseph Inslee. It is retangular in shape with a low hip roof and a symmetrical three-bay façade with triangular shaped and moulded stone lintels. The porch was added ca. 1915. It fills the façade and is supported by tapering wood posts resting on brick piers.

614 N. 4th

A single-story brick Italianate house built ca. 1880 for Walter Sanders. It features a truncated hip roof with boxed cornices and has a two bay façade. The first bay is dominated by a chamfer-shaped bay window with segmental arched window bays in all three facets. The original porch has been removed.

608 N. 6th

A rectangular two-story brick Italianate house, this was built as a twin to 602 N. 6th. A flat roof lies bhind the façade parapet with three symmetrical bays at either story. The façade openings have raised brick surrounds feature keystone accents.
